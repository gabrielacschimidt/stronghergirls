The Project

StrongHER Girls™: Coaching & Personal Training will teach you how to be strong, from the inside-out. We give you the tools to empower yourself through remote and in-person training.


We provide a unique, personalized approach to high-quality, goal-oriented training. Our team of Coaches is educated, qualified, and capable. You will work with your Coach, who will create a program that will help you progress toward your training goals. Your Coach will empower you with the confidence and knowledge to achieve your fitness dreams. Whether you want to gain muscle, lose excess fat, or learn how to maintain an active and healthy lifestyle, StrongHER Girls™: Coaching & Personal Training designs your program to your fitness and wellness goals. Working together with your Coach, you will discuss your personal goals and timeline in order to devise a plan of action with short-term and long-term goals.

The Movement

Based in Brooklyn to uplift girls, womxn, and folx of marginalized identities.


StrongHER Girls™: The Movement is dedicated to teaching underserved girls, womxn, and folx of marginalized identities about strengthening their bodies, from the inside-out. StrongHER Girls™: The Movement was founded by Salma Nakhlawi, a competitive powerlifter, to empower girls, womxn, and folx of marginalized identities through strength. Raised by an immigrant mother, Sal was taught by her mother to challenge mediocrity and self-doubt. At StrongHER Girls™: The Movement, we help girls of color find their voice through an intersectional feminist curriculum dedicated to boost self-esteem, self-image, challenging gender and societal ideals, and teaching the power and olympic lifts. In short, StrongHER Girls™ is dedicated to teaching girls of color to uplift each other and lift heavyass weight.

Girls are strong and we’re only getting StrongHER.

Why do we use the word “womxn”?

We use the word WOMXN because it is a more inclusive, intersectional term that sheds light on the prejudice, discrimination, and institutional barriers that womxn have faced.

It also shows that womxn are not extensions of men, but are their own free and separate entities.

This term is also more inclusive than terms such as “woman”, “women”, and “womyn” because it includes non-binary folx, trans-womxn, and womxn of color.
